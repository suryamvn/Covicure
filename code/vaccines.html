    • What is a vaccine

    A vaccine is a biological preparation that provides active acquired immunity to a particular infectious disease.[1] A vaccine typically contains an agent that resembles a disease-causing microorganism and is often made from weakened or killed forms of the microbe, its toxins, or one of its surface proteins.

    Importance of vaccine

  • The vaccine reduces your risk of infection.
  • The vaccine protects against severe illness.
  • The vaccine helped us ditch the mask.
  • The vaccine will help you reconnect with friends and family.

    • About Covaxin

    COVAXIN®, India's indigenous COVID-19 vaccine by Bharat Biotech is developed in collaboration with the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) - National Institute of Virology (NIV). The indigenous, inactivated vaccine is developed and manufactured in Bharat Biotech's BSL-3 (Bio-Safety Level 3) high containment facility.

    ㅤ

    COVAXIN® is included along with immune-potentiators, also known as vaccine adjuvants, which are added to the vaccine to increase and boost its immunogenicity.

    ㅤ

    It is a 2-dose vaccination regimen given 28 days apart.

    About Covishield

    The Oxford–AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccine, codenamed AZD1222,[5] and sold under the brand names Covishield[19] and Vaxzevria[1][20] among others, is a viral vector vaccine for prevention of COVID-19.

    ㅤ

    Developed by Oxford University and AstraZeneca, it is given by intramuscular injection, using as a vector the modified chimpanzee adenovirus ChAdOx1.

    ㅤ

    Studies carried out in 2020 showed that the efficacy of the vaccine is 76.0% at preventing symptomatic COVID-19 beginning at 22 days following the first dose and 81.3% after the second dose.

    ㅤ

    The initial course consists of two doses with an interval of 4 to 12 weeks between doses. 

    ㅤ

    The World Health Organization (WHO) recommends an interval of 8 to 12 weeks between doses for optimal efficacy
